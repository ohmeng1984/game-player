Mobile Legends Bang Bang Review

FGR

By Dejan Kacurov November 23, 2016

Game Info:

Summary: Mobile Legends Bang Bang is a free to play mobile game on Play Store and App Store with MOBA elements.

Developer: Moonton

Genre(s): Strategy, Turn-Based, General

Cheats: GameFAQs

Mobile Legends Bang Bang is a mobile game with MOBA elements. Recently I have downloaded the game, so I will try to make a good review, with full gameplay and all the heroes in Mobile Legends Bang Bang.

The game is free to play on Play Store and App Store. I use Android system, so this review can be helpful for Android users. At this moment there are more than 10 millions of downloads of the game with 4.5 score from 500.000 players. The content of the game is ok for kids 12+.

Game Play

When you start, first you will write your alias. Then you start your first match. This first match it’s only a practice, so don’t get hyped if you kill all enemies and finish the match in 2 minutes. And don’t think that Mobile Legends is a simple, based on an MOBA game, because you will feel the pain in matches 5 vs 5.

After the practice match, there will be a guide that shows you where you can purchase items, heroes and gear. You will choose a hero. A tank, fighter, assassin, mage, marksman or support. There are 19 heroes, but not all of them are available. Leveling up, getting experience and gold, will provide you to buy different heroes later. In the tank class you have 4 heroes, Tigreal, Minotaur, Lolita and Akai. Fighters are Bane, Balmond, Alucard and Franco. The sneaky assassins are Saber, Fanny, Hayabusa and Karina. Mage group Nana, Alice and Eudora. There are 3 Marksman Miya, Clint and Bruno. And the last hero, the support is Rafaela.

Each hero has different abilities, depends on their type. Also, heroes in Mobile Legends are with 3 skills, 2 starters and 1 ultimate. These skills can be learned during gameplay.

After few matches on Match Up mode in Mobile Legends, you will enter the Brawl mode. This is a 5 vs 5 mode without a lane. You have to destroy two towers to win the Brawl. After winning you will be awarded medals. A great chance to improve your gameplay skills.

Main Goal in Mobile Legends

In the game, there are 3 lanes. Top, middle and bottom lane. You can level up by killing minions and your opponents. The main goal is to push together with your team, destroy the 3 towers on a lane and finish the match by destroying the “Enemy’s Crystal” to win.

Recently the game was updated with a new hero, Freya, a chat mode, hero balance, new skins and single lane 5 vs 5.

I do recommend this game, because I’m a huge fan of MMORPG games and Mobile Legends is very close to them. It is a fun game, very addictive and simple.

Rules of Survival Review

PocketGamer

By Harry Slater November 22, 2017

Game Info:

Summary: Now all the Battle Royale fans have a chance explore a brand new Multiplayer Shooter game called Rules of Survival which has just been released. The game was created by NetEase games, and greatly inspired by PlayerUnknown’s Battlegrounds game, but it still has its own charms. Rules of Survival game is currently downloadable on mobile platforms. The Android users are able to freely download it to their devices.

Developer: NetEase Game

Genre(s): Action Adventure, Survival

Cheats: GameFAQs

PlayerUnknown's BattleGrounds is all the talk in PC gaming circles at the moment. It's a massive deathmatch that sees you trying to be the last person surviving on an island full of other human-controlled avatars.

So it's hardly a surprise to see the formula being lifted wholesale for a mobile game. And that's exactly what Rules of Survival does. It's a battle royale. It's on an island. And you're dropped into the game wearing a vest and some trousers. But does it manage to capture the formula that's been so successful on PC, and that Microsoft hopes is going to do so well for it in the future? Eh, no, not really.

Bang Bang

The first thing you're going to notice about the game is how badly translated it is. Almost every text box that pops up feels like it's been threaded through Google Translate about eight times before it's got to your eyes.

The whole thing feels sluggish too. It crashes, it chugs, and it takes you a good long while to figure out just how to get into a game.

When you do though, things start looking up. Or at least, they start looking up a little bit. After all, this is a third-person shooter dropped onto mobile. In other words, if you're daunted by buttons on screen, it's likely to set you frothing at the mouth.

There are a good deal of problems with the actual gameplay. Moving is awkward, shooting is awkward, and if you're playing on a device with a small screen then, quite frankly, everything is pretty awkward.

But the game still manages to capture the tension inherent in its format. You're not just trying to kill, you're trying to stay alive. Hiding in a barn has never felt quite so intense on mobile, and Rules of Survival should be championed for that.

Then you'll get shot in the back of the head and instead of returning fire you'll just sort of jump up and down on the spot. And when you die the game will probably crash and you won't get any of the rewards you earned.

Cash Landing

So is Rules of Survival worth your time? I mean, it's a pretty hard sell. It's fudgey and broken when it needs to be smooth and intuitive, and that translation is enough to put anyone off after about ten seconds of reading.

But if you want to play PUBG on mobile, well you might be able to look past that. If you're on the fence, then I'd suggest giving this one a couple of months to iron out its multifaceted kinks before you dive in.

Pokemon Go Review

Good Housekeeping

By Lewis Heayns July 2016

Game Info:

Summary: Venusaur, Charizard, Blastoise, Pikachu, and many other Pokémon have been discovered on planet Earth. Now’s your chance to discover and capture the Pokémon all around you—so get your shoes on, step outside, and explore the world. You’ll join one of three teams and battle for the prestige and ownership of Gyms with your Pokémon at your side.

Developer: Niantic Labs

Genre(s): Role-Playing, Trainer

Cheats: GameFAQs

It has been just over 20 years since the original Pokémon Red and Blue games hit the shelves but The Pokémon Company, in partnership with Niantic Labs, have just released their most revolutionary game yet: a free app available on iOS and Android devices called Pokémon GO. The game turns the streets in your town into your own Pokémon universe. The goal is the same — you "gotta catch em' all" — but instead of sitting inside on your Game Boy, you have to head out and explore your neighborhood to catch Pokémon, hatch eggs, and capture Gyms.

After you customize your trainer, you are faced with the always-difficult decision of which starter Pokémon to choose. Once you weigh up the pros and cons of Squirtle, Charmander and Bulbasaur, you are shown how you catch Pokémon in this universe — flick Pokeballs at them until they get tired.

As you explore the ever-changing landscape, you will come across a wide-variety of Pokémon, depending on your location. Grass Pokémon appear in grassy areas and water Pokémon near rivers, lakes and oceans. You can see what varieties are nearby and are told how close you are with helpful step icons, which means you can really hunt the Pokémon you want.

By catching lots of the same breed, you can collect the specific Candy needed to evolve your Pokémon, and cash in duplicates to get more Candy. Just like in the original games, you hatch your Pokémon Eggs by traveling a certain distance, but this time you actually have to walk that distance — getting real exercise.

The developers have done a great job turning your local sites and attractions into what they call Pokéstops, where you can collect extra Pokéballs, Pokémon Eggs, and other helpful items. Once you reach a certain level, you get to choose to join one of three teams and work with them to defend and capture gyms in your area.

Although the app is free, Pokémon GO offers in-app purchases to help you in your quest to be a Pokémon Master. You can exchange real-world currency for Pokécoins, which can then be traded for extra items such as Pokéballs, Incense (to attract Pokémon to your location), and "Lucky" Eggs.

To fully immerse yourself in the Pokémon world, you will soon be able to purchase the Pokémon Go Plus accessory (available in late July 2016). This portable device will connect via Bluetooth and notify the player of any in-game activity nearby. The player will then be able to catch Pokémon and perform other simple actions, without having to take out their phone.

So what's the downside to this already crazy-popular game? Its server "issues" have stopped many eager fans from hitting the streets in search of Pokémon, and the app still has some bugs that need working out.

Bottom line: The Good Housekeeping Institute loves this novel approach to combat inactivity in the video game generation. Even though the app still has some issues, this game will definitely be a massive hit this summer.

Also, a very important safety tip: Please, DO NOT Pokémon Go and drive. Also, be very careful where you're wandering around, too. There are already reports of accidents and some grim discoveries.
